Our First Hackathon

Posted by IoT Club on February 26, 2017

Our Hackathon Project on Github

With over 2,000 participants, the DeveloperWeek Hackathon is amongst one of the largest in the nation. Our IoT club sent a team to compete in this event, not expecting much seeing as that is was our very first hackathon. We arrived excited albeit a bit nervous seemingly surrounded by experienced adult programmers.

We settled in and discussed which API from which sponsor we should use, initially joking that we would unrealistically go for the highest cash prize, which would mean the most competition. After considering our options, we decided to go with PubNub Civic Hack Challenge. Our goal was to create an application that would help the community or government. To address this, we created EZFeedback, a real-time in-class feedback system helping students and teachers optimize their class time, especially in lectures.

During our second and final day, we gave our pitch to the judges. Right before the top 5 projects were being announced our team had been frantically coding to the last minute, and having missed lunch, we decided to run out for food. Deciding that we had little to no chance winning a prize, our team decided to take their time and eat a proper lunch at California Pizza Kitchen. As we sat down, one of our members received a phone call telling us that we had made the top 5 projects and had 10 minutes to set up for our presentation. Having taken many scam calls, and the audio being drowned out by the noise of the restaurant, he simply replied “sorry, you have the wrong number” and hung up. A minute later, the same member read his e-mails one repeating the same message, and told his team, multiple times due to their confusion and disbelief. We ended up winning 2nd place far surpassing expectations for any high school team.
