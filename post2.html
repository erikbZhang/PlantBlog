Watermelon Harvest

September 2, 2017

I was excited after seeing my figs do so well (check that post out here) and was ecstatic about my other plants.

While watering my plants, I thought my watermelons looked quite ripe. I repeatedly tapped them like my parents taught me to, and I thought the watermelon sounded very hollow, signifying that it was ripe. After doing this for a week, I was convinced it was ready. California is so hot, and my mouth would definitely appreciate some cold, juicy watermelon.

So, I decided to take a leap of faith and cut the watermelon. With high hopes, I skipped back to the house with a watermelon in my arms like I was Frank Gore.

As soon as I got home I grabbed a knife. What I saw was very suprising. The watermelon was all white, with just a spot of red in the middle. Maybe I should do some more research next time. According to Google, a watermelon takes about 120 days to ripen, so I was about 75 days early. Slighly premature from me.

I guess the figs were beginners luck. We'll see how the remaining plants fare.
